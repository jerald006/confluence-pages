This document gives brief on how commissions earned are calculated on Xeni platform.

Components

There are 5 components of a price. Out of these 2 are calculated values.

Cost

The amount at which a given counter-party receives a service is the Cost.

If a Vendor provides a particular service to Xeni for $123, then Xeni Cost would be $123.

Similarly, since Xeni is providing the service to Agency Owners, the price at which Xeni sells its service to an agency after adding commission becomes the Agency Cost.

Markup

The percentage(%) of profit that a given counter-party wants to earn for a given service. The markup is applied to the Cost Price.

For example, the Cost Price of a service is $150 and the counter-party chooses to add a Markup of 5%. The Markup Percentage is 5% and the Markup Amount is 7.5.

The price at which the service is now sold becomes $107.5.

Each counter-party can change the markup of any service that they want. They can change is for all the receiving parties at a global level. Or, they can change it for individual receiver as well.

For example, Xeni can charge a markup of 5% for every agency owner, and Xeni can charge a markup of 2% for specific Agency Owner.

An agency owner can charge a markup of 5% for all sales agent and customers, or 3% for specific Sales Agents.

Selling Price (Cost + Markup Amount)

The final price at which a counter-party sells a service to its receiving parties. This is calculated by adding the price at which the counter-party receives a service and the amount they want to receive in profit.

For example, if the Cost Price to Xeni is $150 and it adds a markup of 5%, the Selling Price of the service becomes $107.5. This now becomes the Cost Price for the receiving parties. So, Agency Cost Price = Xeni Selling Price.

Transaction Fee

The fees that the payment gateway charges on a particular transaction. Since each payment gateway has a different transaction fee, the amount charged for different payment gateway will vary.

Xeni pays the transaction fees on its selling price. So, if Xeni is offering a service for $110 and the Transaction charge for the gateway is 1%, the transaction fee for Xeni is $1.10.

Agency Owner pays the transaction fees on their markup value. So, if the agency owner is earning a profit of $10, and the Transaction charge of the payment gateway is 1%, the transaction fee for the Agency Owner is $0.1.

Commission (Markup Amount - Transaction Fee)

The total commission that a counter-party earns after in commission after deducting the transaction fee.

Payment Gateway Charge

ConnexPay - 3%

Stripe - 3%

BitPay - 1%
