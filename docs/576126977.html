XeniApp : Postmortem: Xeni wordpress down due to DDOS Attack

Created by Kavibritto Chalaman, last modified on Sep 25, 2023

Xeni WordPress downtime due DDOS attack performed by unknown hacker

Date: 21 Sep 2023

Authors: Kavibritto Chalaman

Status: InProgress

Summary: Xeni WordPress website was down due DDOS attack. During this period xeni.com was not available but xeni booking engine was working fine.

Impact: Potential revenue loss.

Root Causes:

  • On September 21, 2023, at 2:27:30 PM EDT, Sachin received an email from an unknown hacker. The email claimed that the attacker had identified a vulnerability on the xeni.com/sign-up page. In the email, the attacker threatened to take down the website for at least a month unless a ransom of $5000 USD was paid.

  • Following the receipt of this email, the xeni.com WordPress website experienced a significant disruption in its services. A Zoom call was initiated on September 21, 2023, at 3:45:00 PM EDT, to assess the situation.

  • During the Zoom call, it was observed that the website was inaccessible, and users attempting to access xeni.com were greeted with a 502 Bad Gateway error on their browsers.

  • Infrastructure analysis revealed that both the Nginx and Apache2 servers were overwhelmed with a substantial number of incoming requests, resulting in server CPU utilisation constantly hitting 100%. This high resource utilisation was attributed to the alleged DDoS attack.

Resolution:

The following Action will take to resolve this issue.

  • Upon confirming the severity of the incident during the Zoom call on September 21, 2023, at 4:00:00 PM EDT, the following immediate actions were taken to mitigate the impact of the DDoS attack and address the reported vulnerabilities:

    • Stopped the web server and database server to halt ongoing attacks and prevent further damage to the infrastructure.

    • Created new AWS EC2 instances and RDS instances from backups to restore the website to a known good state.

    • Applied AWS Web Application Firewall (WAF) to the CloudFront endpoint to filter incoming traffic and block malicious requests.

    • Enabled CAPTCHA and challenge-based rules in the AWS WAF to protect against automated bot traffic and further DDoS attempts.

    • then attached the isolated instance to the main CloudFront endpoint

These actions helped restore the website's functionality and put measures in place to enhance its security.
